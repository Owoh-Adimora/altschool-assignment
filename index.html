About Me

Biography

Name: Owoh Adimora

Early Life and Education

I was born on June 16, 1996, in Okunino village, Udi town, Enugu state, Nigeria. Growing up in a modest household, I exhibited an early curiosity for learning. My parents nurtured my inquisitive nature, fostering an environment where questions were encouraged, and knowledge was celebrated.

I completed my O'level education at "Good Shepherd Anglican Seminary, Enugu", where I excelled in academics and extracurricular activities. I finished O'level in 2015 and gained admission to the Institute of Management and Technology Enugu, where I studied mass communication.

Academic and Professional Achievements

At the Institute of Management and Technology (IMT) Enugu, I pursued a diploma in Mass Communication. My undergraduate years were marked by impressive accomplishments, including representing my department at the Students Union Government (SUG) level as the Deputy Speaker of the SUG Parliament.

After completing my Higher National Diploma (HND) in Mass Communication, I chose a path in tech and am currently studying Software Engineering at AltSchool Africa.

Reason for Joining the School

I chose to join AltSchool Africa because of its excellent reputation in engineering and its commitment to fostering a culture of creativity and problem-solving. I have always had a passion for technology and innovation, and from the information gathered about AltSchool Africa, I believe I stand a chance to enhance my skills towards tech to provide solutions to issues facing society at large through technology.

Though I have no background knowledge of tech or anything related to tech, as my core discipline is Communication Art (specifically Mass Communication), seeing how the world has evolved towards tech, I decided to opt for software engineering. The best and easiest way to achieve my goal is by enrolling in a credible and well-profiled institution like AltSchool Africa.

Goals for the Engineering Program

My primary goal in the engineering program is to develop a deep understanding of Software Engineering.

I also aim to gain hands-on experience through internships and projects and to contribute to innovative research in front-end engineering. Ultimately, I hope to use my skills to make a positive impact in the world by developing software and applications to help solve the everyday problems people face.
